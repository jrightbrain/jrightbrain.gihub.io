Sustainability is not a score card—it’s about doing the right and necessary thing for our clients, their campuses and our future.

Did you know that green buildings:

Support teaching and learning
Green buildings are teaching buildings Not only can they be integrated with numerous lesson plans, but they create healthy environments that encourage learning:
  • Daylight and views increase performance
  • High indoor air quality improves health, and therefore good attendance
  • Acoustics increase learning potential
  • Thermal comfort increases occupant satisfaction
Source-http://www.buildgreenschools.org/resources/research.html

Add to your bottom line
Imagine what you could do with the money you save with more efficient energy systems.

Worried about the upfront investment of a green building? Davis Langdon’s July 2007 report Cost of Green Revisited found “there is no significant difference in average cost for green buildings as compared to non-green buildings.”

Source-http://www.davislangdon.com/USA/Research/ResearchFinder/2007-The-Cost-of-Green-Revisited/

Address larger global issues
Green buildings are an outward commitment to good stewardship. According to the USGBC, the built environment has a profound impact on our natural environment, economy, health and productivity. In the United States alone, buildings account for:
  • 72 percent of electricity consumption
  • 39 percent of energy use
  • 38 percent of all carbon dioxide (CO2) emissions
  • 40 percent of raw materials use
  • 30 percent of waste output (136 million tons annually)
  • 14 percent of potable water consumption.
Are on the rise
The three largest segments for nonresidential green building construction—office, education and health care—will account for more than 80 percent of total nonresidential green construction in 2008. [source: FMI (2008). U.S. Construction Overview]
