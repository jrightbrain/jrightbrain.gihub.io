With an emphasis on health, aesthetics, operation and maintenance cost savings, Central Michigan University’s challenge was to design a landmark building that showcases its College of Education and harmonizes with existing campus structures. To support their sustainable investment, SHW developed an energy model to calculate how much the building will save annually with its potential LEED gold education building. They will save nearly $1 million in electricity costs and more than 15 million gallons of water over 30 years.
